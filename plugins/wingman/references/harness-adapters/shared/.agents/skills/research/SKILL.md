# Research

Turns a vague founder question into a sourced, plain-language brief with
citations, so decisions are made on evidence rather than guesswork.

## When to use
- The founder wants evidence before a decision (market, tech, legal, process).
- Comparing approaches across vendors or the literature.
- A claim needs a source before it enters a plan or doc.

## Method
1. Decompose the question into 2-4 sub-questions.
2. Use web search and targeted reads; prefer primary sources and vendor docs.
3. Synthesize a brief: claim → evidence → source link → confidence.
4. Lead with the founder-relevant consequence, not the mechanism.
5. Separate "what we found" from "what we recommend."

## Output shape
- One-paragraph bottom line.
- Bulleted findings, each with a source.
- A confidence note (high/medium/low) and any gaps.

## Rationalizations
- "I already know the answer." — Verify; an unverified answer is a guess.
- "A source roughly supports it." — Cite precisely; vague citations mislead.
- "More search will perfect it." — Bound the loop; report confidence, not false certainty.

## Red Flags
- Fabricated or unreachable citations — never invent sources.
- Presenting a single source as consensus.
- Mixing findings with recommendation without labeling which is which.

## Verification
Every factual claim in the brief maps to a real, reachable source you actually
opened. Re-read the brief and confirm no claim is uncited and no secret was
exposed in the process.
